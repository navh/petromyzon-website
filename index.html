About Us

We are developing technology to capture the most accurate models of underwater structures.

Pairing Sonar with neuromorphic hardware enables AI workloads to be run as close to the sensor as possible. This offers higher bandwidth, more signal, less noise, and lower power consumption. By running networks that can Detect, Locate, Classify, and Track underwater contacts we create a real-time 3D model of the underwater environment.

Toronto's proximity to the St. Lawrence Seaway gives us access to a wide range of underwater structures in fresh water for testing and development.

Our Founders

Murat Isik received his B.Sc. degree in Electrical and Electronics Engineering from Turkey in 2021, followed by an M.Sc. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Drexel University, Philadelphia, USA, in 2023. He is currently pursuing a PhD in Electrical Engineering at Stanford University, focusing on energy estimation projects and their applications. He also holds a research scholarship at SLAC National Accelerator Laboratory, where he continues his work on energy estimation. His contributions to academia include awards from organizations such as IEEE, APS, ACM, Foresight, and the Howard Brain Science Foundation. Along with an extensive portfolio of published research, Murat's interests lie in Neuromorphic Hardware and the development of novel architectures for machine learning acceleration. His work has earned recognition from institutions like the NSF and DOE, as well as grant support for his innovative research.

Amos Hebb received his B.Eng. degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the Royal Military College of Canada in 2019, with a capstone project using neural networks for sensor fusion in a SLAM pipeline, and an M.A.Sc. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of Toronto, focusing on scheduling of mixed sensors for searching and tracking targets with reinforcement learning. He has operational experience on CP-140, Boeing P-8, and Kawasaki P-1. He runs underwater photogrammetry courses and dives year round in Lake Ontario and the Bay of Fundy.
